Whispers of the Flute

Carved in ink, yet born of sound,
A flute lies still on sacred ground.
No lips to breathe, no hands to play,
Yet songs within it softly stay.
Adorned with swirls and mandala light,
A silent echo of pure delight.
Its patterns sing of ancient lore,
Of love, of peace, of evermore.
Leaf and stem like rhythm flow,
As winds of grace around it blow.
Nature listens, hushed and kind,
To the music sketched in line and mind.
It needs no color, voice, or fame,
Its beauty hums without a name.
A whisper drawn in black and white,
A melody caught in morning light.
