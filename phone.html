The Story Behind

With turban tied and eyes gone wide, He held his phone up to the side. A puzzled look, a raised brow tight, This wasn’t going quite so right. "Hello?" he asked, with growing fear, "But what you say, I cannot hear!" The signal weak, the words unclear, He poked his arm to check — “Is it here?” He flipped the phone, he gave a shake, As if the fault was some mistake. His shirt was torn, his patience thin, This call was not a lucky win. “Recharge I did! Just yesterday!” He mumbled loud in disarray. “But now this thing just mocks my fate— And eats my balance while I wait!” Poor man, just trying to call his son, Who said he’d come by half-past one. But all he got was static tone, And the echo of a dial tone. A tale as old as time, you see, Of tech and man in rivalry. In every town, on every street— Someone’s stuck in this same repeat.
